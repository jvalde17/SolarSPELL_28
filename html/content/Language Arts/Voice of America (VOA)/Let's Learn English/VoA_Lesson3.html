Lesson 3: I'm Here!

Objectives:

Students practice giving a telephone number and calling someone they know.

Students practice giving addresses.

Students learn to use the strategy, Look for Ways to Practice.

Speaking Practice

Pronunciation Practice

Conversation

Anna: I am in my new apartment! Great!

Anna: I live with Marsha. We're roommates.

Anna: I want to cook dinner. Oh! Hmmm. Is there a supermarket near here?

Anna: Marsha knows. Marsha's work number is 555-8986.

Man: Hello.

Anna: Is this Marsha? It is Anna.

Man: I am sorry. You have the wrong number.

Anna: Oh. This is 555-8686?

Man: No. This is 555-8689.

Anna: Oh. Excuse me!

Man: Okay. 'Bye.

Anna: One more time. 555-8686. Please be Marsha.

Marsha: Hello. This is Marsha.

Anna: Yes, Marsha. I want to cook dinner.

Marsha: Excuse me? Anna? Is that you?

Anna: Oh, yes. I am am here.

Marsha: Good. You are there.

Anna: I want to find a supermarket.

Marsha: Oh, okay. The supermarket is at 1500 Irving Street. It is near the apartment.

Anna: Great! Goodbye!

Marsha: Goodbye, Anna. (Later)

Anna: There is a big supermarket on our street. Ans Marsha says I am a good cook! Until next time! Bye!

New Words

cook - v. to prepare (food) for eating specially by using heat

dinner - n. the main meal of the day

excuse - v.  to forgive someone for making a mistake or doing something wrong 

find - v. to get or discover something or someone that you are looking for

here - adv. in this place or at this location 

live - v. to have a home in a specified place

near - adv. close to something or someone 

sorry - adj. feeling sorrow or regret 

supermarket - n. store where customers can buy a variety of foods and household items

there - adv. in that that place or in that location

want - v. to desire or wish for something

wrong - adj. not suitable or appropriate for a particular purpose, situation, or person
